About

Hello! I'm Viki, a first-year Astrophysics PhD student at the University of Hertfordshire. I study the atmospheres of benchmark brown dwarfs in the L–T transition using the Brewster atmospheric retrieval framework under the supervision of Dr. Ben Burningham. These brown dwarfs, observed with JWST NIRSpec and MIRI, are widely separated companions to FGK stars with well-characterised compositions—providing a unique opportunity to explore how silicate clouds shape their near- and mid-infrared spectra and to test cloud formation models.

In addition to my research, I currently work as a Visiting Lecturer at the University of Hertfordshire, where I lead computer lab sessions for MSc Data Science students.

Before moving to the UK, I completed an MSc in Astronomy Research at Leiden University in the Netherlands, and a BSc in Earth Sciences at Eötvös Loránd University in Hungary.

During my BSc, I was an Observatory Assistant at the Konkoly Observatory, conducting regular observations at the Piszkéstető Mountain Station.

Research

  • Brown Dwarf Atmospheric Retrievals (Ongoing)

    Analysing low-resolution JWST NIRSPec and MIRI spectra of benchmark L-T dwarfs using the Brewster atmospheric retrieval framework.

    • Learn More

  • Exoplanet Thermal Evolution (Second MSc Thesis)

    Investigated how thermal evolution affects atmospheric composition and transmission spectra of H₂-rich exoplanets, with implications for CO₂ feature detection.

    • Learn More

  • Structure of Protoplanetary Disks (First MSc Thesis)

    Analysed VLTI-MATISSE data to model inner disk substructure and identify potential planet-induced gaps in Herbig Ae/Be and T Tauri stars.

    • Learn More

  • K2 Lightcurves of Asteroids (BSc Project)

    Modeled light curves of Hilda asteroids and transneptunian objects from Kepler K2 data to derive shapes, densities, rotation periods, and contact binary candidates.
